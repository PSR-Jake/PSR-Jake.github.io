Research Highlights

Compact objects, resolved carefully

My research combines detailed observational analysis with broader astrophysical questions about how compact objects form, evolve, and cluster.

Millisecond pulsars in globular clusters

Globular clusters are ideal environments for studying MSP populations because their high stellar densities efficiently produce the binaries that spin neutron stars up to millisecond periods.

Much of my work focuses on building a clearer observational picture of these systems in X-rays and connecting them to their cluster environments.

From cluster cores to the Galactic bulge

I am interested in how compact-object studies in one environment inform another, especially when it comes to unresolved MSP populations and the Galactic Center gamma-ray excess.

That same curiosity extends to magnetars and the newly emerging long-period transient population.

Omega Centauri as a case study

A recurring thread in my work is how X-ray observations can help isolate faint compact systems in crowded cluster fields.

Optical and Chandra views of Omega Centauri offer a glimpse into the environments where these questions become concrete.
